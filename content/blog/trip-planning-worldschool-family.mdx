# How to Plan a Year of Worldschooling: A Practical Guide

Planning a year of travel with kids sounds overwhelming — but with the right framework, it becomes one of the most exciting projects your family will ever tackle together.

## Step 1: Choose Your Anchors

Most worldschooling families find it works best to choose 2–4 "anchor" cities where you stay for 2–3 months, rather than moving every few weeks. Longer stays mean:

- Kids develop real friendships
- You find the good supermarkets, parks, and playgrounds
- Visa stays are typically well within tourist limits
- Costs drop significantly (monthly rates beat weekly rates)

## Step 2: Consider School Terms

If your kids are still enrolled in an online school or following a curriculum, aligning your moves with school terms can reduce disruption. Popular approaches:

- **Australian school year** (Feb–Dec): Great for Southern Hemisphere or Asia itineraries
- **UK school year** (Sept–Jul): Aligns well with Europe and UK-based curricula
- **Unschooling**: No term constraints — follow your curiosity

## Step 3: Stack Your Visa Durations

Tourist visas typically allow 30–90 days. Plan your stays to:

1. Stay within tourist limits wherever possible
2. Use visa runs strategically (e.g., Thailand 30 days → Laos border run → 30 more days)
3. Research family visas for longer stays (Portugal D7, Mexico Temporary Resident, etc.)

## Step 4: Build a Budget Buffer

The golden rule: budget for 20% more than you think you'll spend. Unexpected costs — medical, flights, accommodation gaps — are the norm, not the exception.
